# VOD Fest 2026 - Band Bios (Entwurf)

## FRIDAY, 17 July

### 1. JOACHIM IRMLER (FAUST) — Outside Stage, 18:00-18:30

Hans Joachim Irmler is a founding member of Faust, one of the most influential krautrock groups in music history. Formed in 1971 in Wumme, Lower Saxony, Faust pioneered radical approaches to studio recording and live electronics. Irmler built his own custom organ and co-designed the band's signature "black box" effects units. Key albums include the debut Faust (1971), The Faust Tapes (1973) — famously sold at single price on Virgin Records — and Faust IV (1973).

Irmler has continued performing and recording as a solo artist and collaborator, releasing material on the Klangbad and Staubgold labels. He performs on the Outside Stage on Friday, July 17, from 18:00 to 18:30.

### 2. NO MORE — Outside Stage, 19:30-20:30

No More is a German post-punk and dark wave band founded in the summer of 1979 in Kiel. The original lineup consisted of Andy A. Schwarz, Tina Sanudakura, Christian Darc, and Thomas Welz. Their self-released debut EP "Too Late" appeared in 1980, followed by the widely circulated single "Suicide Commando" in 1981 and the mini-LP "A Rose Is A Rose" in 1982, which the NME compared to Lou Reed's Berlin.

The band dissolved in 1986 after completing the album Hysteria. Schwarz and Sanudakura reconvened as a duo in 2006 with the album Remake/Remodel and have continued performing since. No More play the Outside Stage on Friday, July 17, from 19:30 to 20:30.

### 3. Ditterich von Euler-Donnersperg — Outside Stage, 21:30-22:15

Ditterich von Euler-Donnersperg is the performance alias of Uli Rehberg, a Hamburg-based artist and label founder active in Germany's industrial avant-garde since the early 1980s. In 1980, Rehberg founded Walter Ulbricht Schallfolien, a label that provided early releases for SPK, Laibach, and Werkbund. Under his alias, he has released yearly booklets paired with electronic sound pieces since 1996.

His collaborators have included John Duncan, Thomas Köner, and Column One. His work spans electroacoustic investigation, noise, and sound art. He performs on the Outside Stage on Friday, July 17, from 21:30 to 22:15.

### 4. IRSOL — Venue Inside, 17:00-18:00

IRSOL was a minimal synth group from Coventry, England, active in the early 1980s. The band released two cassettes — First Contact (1982) and Half Life (1983) — on Adventures In Reality Recordings, Alan Rider's Coventry-based industrial and experimental label that also released material by SPK and Test Department. Their recordings were made at Black Prince Studios between 1981 and 1983.

IRSOL's sound sits within the minimal synth and post-industrial electronic scene that emerged from the UK's DIY cassette culture. They perform at the Venue Inside on Friday, July 17, from 17:00 to 18:00.

---

### 5. O YUKI CONJUGATE — Venue Inside, 18:30-19:30

O Yuki Conjugate is an English ambient and post-industrial group founded in 1982 in Nottingham by Roger Horberry and Andrew Hulme. Emerging from the experimental wing of post-punk, they pioneered what they call "dirty ambient" — atmospheric soundscapes combining organic textures with electronic elements. Key albums include Scene in Mirage (1984), Into Dark Water (1987), Peyote (1990), and Equator (1995), all of which have gained retrospective critical acclaim.

Now in their fourth incarnation, the group remains intermittently active around the core of Horberry and Hulme. O Yuki Conjugate perform at the Venue Inside on Friday, July 17, from 18:30 to 19:30.

---

### 6. CRASH COURSE IN SCIENCE — Venue Inside, 20:30-21:30

Crash Course in Science is a minimal synth trio formed in 1979 in Philadelphia by Dale Feliciello, Mallory Yago, and Michael Zodorozny. Meeting at art school, they built their sound using toy instruments, kitchen appliances, distorted guitar, and primitive synthesizers. Their 1981 EP Signals From Pier Thirteen, featuring "Cardboard Lamb" and "Flying Turns," became a staple in adventurous DJ sets and influenced the later electroclash movement.

The album Near Marineland, recorded in 1981, was eventually released via Minimal Wave Records. In 2017, they released Situational Awareness, an album of entirely new material. Crash Course in Science play the Venue Inside on Friday, July 17, from 20:30 to 21:30.

---

### 7. HUNTING LODGE — Venue Inside, 22:45-24:00

Hunting Lodge was an American industrial project founded in May 1982 in Port Huron, Michigan, by Lon C. Diehl, Carla J. Nordstrom, and Richard Skott Rusch. Diehl and Rusch bonded over a shared appreciation for Throbbing Gristle, SPK, and Dome while working at Full Moon Records. Their first live appearance at the Harrington Ballroom in September 1982 was recorded on a portable cassette player and became their debut release.

Key albums include Will (1983) and Nomad Souls, released through the American underground cassette and vinyl network. They were among the earliest DIY electronic industrial acts in the US. Hunting Lodge close out Friday at the Venue Inside, from 22:45 to 24:00.

## SATURDAY, 18 July

### 8. DAS SYNTHETISCHE MISCHGEWEBE — Outside Stage, 18:00-18:45

Das Synthetische Mischgewebe (DSM), meaning "synthetic mixed fabric," is a German experimental and noise project founded in 1980 in Berlin by Guido Hübner. The project emerged from the cassette culture movement and was inspired by industrial and avant-garde music, using custom-made instruments, hacked electronics, and sound installations. Isabelle Chemin was also a member during the project's earlier years.

DSM has collaborated extensively with artists including New Blockaders, Frans de Waard, and Michael Northam, releasing material on labels worldwide. Now the sole project of Hübner, DSM has been active for over four decades. They perform on the Outside Stage on Saturday, July 18, from 18:00 to 18:45.

### 9. MARC HURTADO / LYDIA LUNCH — Outside Stage, 19:45-20:45

This collaboration pairs two formidable figures of the underground. Lydia Lunch, born 1959 in Rochester, New York, was a founding member of the no wave movement through Teenage Jesus and the Jerks and has since built a prolific career in music, spoken word, and film. Marc Hurtado co-founded the French experimental duo Etant Donnes with his brother Eric in 1977 and has collaborated with Alan Vega, Michael Gira, and Genesis P-Orridge.

Since 2019, Lunch and Hurtado have performed the music of Suicide and Alan Vega together, documented on the album Metempsychosis. They perform on the Outside Stage on Saturday, July 18, from 19:45 to 20:45.

### 10. RAPOON — Outside Stage, 21:45-22:45

Rapoon is the solo project of Robin Storey, born 1955 in Cumbria, England. Storey was a founding member of the pioneering industrial collective :zoviet*france: in 1980, contributing to 17 albums over 12 years during what is widely regarded as the group's creative peak. He launched Rapoon in 1992 to explore ethnic music traditions and ambient textures outside his former band's framework.

Early albums Dream Circle (1992) and Fallen Gods (1994) featured hypnotic loops of processed world music rhythms, while later works explored dub, jungle, and isolationist ambiance. Storey is also a noted visual artist. Rapoon performs on the Outside Stage on Saturday, July 18, from 21:45 to 22:45.

### 11. NORMA LOY — Venue Inside, 17:00-18:00

Norma Loy is a French cold wave group formed in 1981 in Dijon by Michel "Usher" Lecamp and Manuel "Chelsea" Calmes. Influenced by Joy Division, Suicide, Cabaret Voltaire, and the No Wave movement, they developed a sound built on restrained guitar, domineering electronics, brass experimentation, and heavily reverberated vocals that became a model for the cold wave genre.

Their first EP appeared in 1983, followed by the album Attitudes (1991). The band went on hiatus in 1993 but reformed in 2006, later releasing Baphomet (2016) and Ouroboros (2023). Norma Loy open Saturday at the Venue Inside, from 17:00 to 18:00.

---

### 12. CLAIR OBSCUR — Venue Inside, 18:45-19:45

Clair Obscur is a French post-punk and cold wave band founded in 1981 in Creil by Thierry Damerval, Christophe Demarthe, and Nicolas Demarthe. Among French cold wave and industrial acts of the 1980s, they achieved some of the widest international recognition, releasing records on UK labels All the Madmen and Cathexis and appearing on the N.E.R. compilation From Torture to Conscience (1984) alongside Current 93 and Death In June.

Their music spans tribal cold wave, chamber music, and performance art. Key releases include In Out (1988) and Rock (1994). The band reactivated in 2004. Clair Obscur perform at the Venue Inside on Saturday, July 18, from 18:45 to 19:45.

---

### 13. THE ANTI GROUP — Venue Inside, 20:45-21:45

The Anti Group (T.A.G.C.) is an experimental multimedia collective founded in the early 1980s by Adi Newton, who also co-founded Clock DVA in 1978 in Sheffield. Newton was briefly a member of The Future, which later became The Human League. T.A.G.C. was conceived as an open-membership research project exploring psychoacoustics, ambisonics, visual art, and performance.

After Clock DVA's 1983 breakup, Newton focused on T.A.G.C., staging performances and releasing several albums of frequency-based experimental work. Clock DVA was reactivated in 1987, but T.A.G.C. has continued as Newton's primary experimental vehicle. The Anti Group perform at the Venue Inside on Saturday, July 18, from 20:45 to 21:45.

---

### 14. ESPLENDOR GEOMETRICO — Venue Inside, 22:45-24:00

Esplendor Geometrico is a Spanish industrial group formed in October 1980 in Madrid by Arturo Lanz, Gabriel Riaza, and Juan Carlos Sastre, all former members of synth-pop act El Aviador Dro. Taking their name from a text by Italian Futurist F.T. Marinetti, they developed a distinctive sound using analog synthesizers and primitive electronic percussion that helped define the rhythm noise genre.

Their debut LP Hero del Trabajo appeared in 1982. They founded their own label in 1985, and key albums include Kosmos Kino (1988) and Sheikh Aljama (1991). Their influence extends into contemporary techno and noise. Esplendor Geometrico close Saturday at the Venue Inside, from 22:45 to 24:00.

## SUNDAY, 19 July

### 15. PLUS INSTRUMENTS — Outside Stage, 18:00-18:45

Plus Instruments is a Dutch experimental project established in 1978 in Eindhoven by Truus de Groot, often called the "Queen of the Dutch Underground." Emerging from the punk explosion, the project evolved toward experimental and industrial music with largely improvised live performances. De Groot collaborated with artists including Lee Ranaldo of Sonic Youth, James Sclavunos, and Michel Waisvisz.

The cult release Februari - April '81, recorded in New York, connected the project to the American No Wave scene. Plus Instruments has remained active across four decades, with recent albums including Trancesonics (2013) and Unnoticed (2026). They perform on the Outside Stage on Sunday, July 19, from 18:00 to 18:45.

---

### 16. PARADE GROUND — Outside Stage, 19:45-20:30

Parade Ground is a Belgian electronic duo formed in 1981 in Brussels by brothers Jean-Marc and Pierre Pauly. Operating at the intersection of cold wave, EBM, and electro-pop, they were closely connected to the Belgian electronic scene — their first 7" single "Moan on the Sly" (1983) was produced by Daniel B. and Patrick Codenys of Front 242.

Their style evolved from icy, radical cold wave toward more rhythmic synthetic compositions, culminating in the album Cut Up (1988), produced with Wire's Colin Newman. After a long hiatus (1988-2007), they returned with The Golden Years compilation (2011) and new material. Parade Ground perform on the Outside Stage on Sunday, July 19, from 19:45 to 20:30.

---

### 17. ETANT DONNES — Outside Stage, 21:30-22:15

Etant Donnes is a French experimental duo founded in 1977 by brothers Eric and Marc Hurtado, born in Morocco. Named after Marcel Duchamp's last major work, their practice spans musique concrete, field recording, performance art, and experimental film. Their debut recording La Vue dates to 1979, and their body of work exceeds 30 albums.

Notable collaborators include Lydia Lunch, Michael Gira, Alan Vega, and Genesis P-Orridge. Key releases include L'Etoile au Front (1982), Bleu (1994), and Re-Up (1999). Their sound combines field recordings, found sounds, and intense vocal delivery. Etant Donnes perform on the Outside Stage on Sunday, July 19, from 21:30 to 22:15.

---

### 18. CLUB MORAL — Venue Inside, 17:00-18:00

Club Moral is a Belgian industrial and noise project founded on January 1, 1981, in Antwerp by visual artist Anne-Mie Van Kerckhoven and performer Danny Devos. Functioning simultaneously as a band and a venue until 1987, Club Moral hosted exhibitions and performances by Boyd Rice, John Duncan, and others while releasing nearly 100 cassettes on their self-run label.

Their own recordings feature raw industrial sound, primitive power electronics, and conceptual art elements. The comprehensive box set Club Moral 1981-86 documents this period. Though declared "dead" by its founders in 2005, the project has continued performing. Club Moral open Sunday at the Venue Inside, from 17:00 to 18:00.

---

### 19. BAND OF HOLY JOY — Venue Inside, 18:45-19:45

The Band of Holy Joy is an English group formed in 1984 in New Cross, South London, by Johny Brown. Their early sound revolved around junk-shop instrumentation and rudimentary electronics, drawing on influences from Jacques Brel, Bertolt Brecht, and European cabaret. After self-issued cassettes, they signed to Flim Flam, then Rough Trade, releasing Manic, Magic, Majestic (1989) and Positively Spooked (1990).

The band dissolved in 1993 but reformed in 2002. Since 2017, they have released a steady stream of albums including Neon Primitives and Scorched Jerusalem (2025). Band of Holy Joy perform at the Venue Inside on Sunday, July 19, from 18:45 to 19:45.

---

### 20. ZOSKIA — Venue Inside, 20:30-21:30

Zos Kia (also credited as Zoskia) is a British project initially formed by John Gosling along with John Balance in 1982. The name derives from the Zos Kia Cultus, a magical system devised by artist Austin Osman Spare. Several 1982-83 performances featured Peter Christopherson on sound, and recordings from this period were released as Transparent (1984) on Nekrophile Records — material that straddles the origins of both Zos Kia and Coil.

After the project's initial phase, Gosling went on to record with Psychic TV and work solo as Mekon. The project is connected to the broader Coil and Psychic TV circle. Zoskia perform at the Venue Inside on Sunday, July 19, from 20:30 to 21:30.

---

### 21. SUTCLIFFE NO MORE — Venue Inside, 22:15-24:00

Sutcliffe No More is an English power electronics and industrial project formed by Kevin Tomkins and Paul Taylor, the founders of Sutcliffe Jugend (est. 1982). After officially ending Sutcliffe Jugend in 2019, the pair continued under this new name, combining spoken word and singing with classical elements, choirs, and electronica while maintaining the intensity of their earlier work.

Albums include Domestic (2021), recorded during lockdown and exploring themes of isolation, as well as House of Dolls, Consenting Adult, and Cute, released on labels including 4iB Records and Zoharum. Sutcliffe No More close the festival at the Venue Inside on Sunday, July 19, from 22:15 to 24:00.
